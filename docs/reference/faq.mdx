Common questions about confidential digital assets and how Ryle provides the infrastructure to issue and operate them.

## What are confidential digital assets?

Confidential digital assets are onchain assets whose balances and transaction amounts are encrypted but remain cryptographically verifiable. They let companies use public-chain settlement without exposing treasury, counterparties, or transaction history. See [what are confidential digital assets](/what-are-confidential-digital-assets).

## What is Ryle?

Ryle is confidential digital asset infrastructure — a platform companies use to issue, manage, and operate confidential assets through a Console for operators and APIs for engineers, while Ryle abstracts the underlying confidential network.

## What is selective disclosure?

Selective disclosure is the ability to reveal specific confidential activity to a chosen party (auditor, regulator, or counterparty) through a scoped, time-bounded, logged view, without making it public. See [selective disclosure](/concepts/selective-disclosure).

## Are confidential digital assets compliant?

Yes. They are private by default but disclosable by policy: issuers can prove reserves and reveal specific activity to regulators and auditors on demand, while every disclosure is logged. See [are confidential digital assets compliant?](/compliance/overview) and how they map to [regulatory frameworks](/compliance/regulatory-frameworks).

## How are confidential assets different from privacy coins?

Privacy coins aim for blanket anonymity. Confidential digital assets are built for regulated business: private by default, but controllable, auditable, and disclosable by policy. See the full breakdown in [confidential assets vs privacy coins](/comparisons/confidential-assets-vs-privacy-coins).

## How do confidential assets stay verifiable if balances are encrypted?

Each transaction carries a zero-knowledge proof that the rules were followed — sufficient balance, conserved supply — so the network verifies validity without seeing the amounts. See [how confidential assets work](/concepts/how-confidential-assets-work).

## What chains do confidential assets settle on?

EVM-compatible chains such as Ethereum, Polygon, Base, Arc, Plasma, and Tempo. The chain target is a per-deployment configuration choice.

## Does Ryle custody funds?

No. Ryle is not a custodian. Partners connect a custody solution they already operate, and Ryle binds the asset to it.

## Can I add confidentiality to an asset I already issue?

Yes. Ryle supports a brand-new confidential asset, a confidential layer on an asset you already issue, or confidentiality on a third-party asset — all behind the same API.

## Who can see an account's balance?

By default, no one but the holder. Operators see aggregate health, not individual balances; external visibility requires an explicit, logged selective disclosure.
