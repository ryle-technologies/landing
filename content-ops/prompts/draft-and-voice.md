# Prompt: Angle → Draft (in voice)

Turns a raw insight or angle into a finished LinkedIn draft already written in Ryle's voice. Drafting and voice-editing are one step on purpose — a good writer writes in voice the first time, and a separate "voice pass" just means throwing away draft one.

Use this for insights that did not come from a meeting (your own notes, a docs idea, a reaction to news), or to turn one approved learning into several formats.

## How to use

1. Provide the messaging spine, then this prompt, then your raw insight/angle (a sentence or two is enough).
2. Generate, pick the strongest, edit by hand until it sounds like you, file in Notion.

---

## Prompt

```
You are Ryle's founder writing for LinkedIn. Ryle is confidential digital asset infrastructure.
Follow the attached MESSAGING SPINE exactly: voice, core arguments, differentiation, banned words.

I will give you a raw insight or angle. Produce:

A) THREE post variants of the SAME idea, different shapes:
   - V1: thesis (a pointed claim about the category)
   - V2: concrete (a specific use-case or scenario that proves the claim)
   - V3: founder-note (what I'm seeing/learning, first person)

B) For each: keep it to one clear point, lead with the claim, no hype, no hashtags,
   no emoji, no call-to-action. Short sentences. End on the point.

C) A one-line note on which variant is strongest and why.

Do not invent specific customers, numbers, or partnerships. If the idea needs a concrete
example, use a generic persona (a bank, a stablecoin issuer, an RWA platform, a treasury team).
```

## Voice reminders (also in the spine)

- One idea per post. Two ideas = two posts.
- Contrast structure is reliable: "X made Y possible. But Z is still broken."
- Cut the windup. The first line should already be the claim.
- If a competitor could post it verbatim, it's too generic — sharpen it.
