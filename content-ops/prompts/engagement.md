# Prompt: Engagement (comment angles)

Given a LinkedIn post from someone in your target world, this generates comment *angles* — not finished comments. You always write the final comment yourself. On LinkedIn, a comment that sounds 5% off to a founder or VC is worse than no comment, and those are exactly the people you are trying to reach.

Replies often produce better leads than original posts. Treat this as a core channel, not an afterthought.

## How to use

1. Provide the messaging spine, then this prompt, then paste the target post text (and who wrote it / their persona).
2. Pick one angle, write the comment in your own words, post it, then log it in Notion (Engagement view / People DB).

---

## Prompt

```
You are advising Ryle's founder on how to engage with a LinkedIn post. Ryle is confidential
digital asset infrastructure. Follow the attached MESSAGING SPINE for perspective and voice.

I will paste a post and the author's persona (e.g. stablecoin founder, RWA platform, crypto VC,
bank/infra). Produce:

1) RELEVANCE: one line — is this worth engaging, and why (does it touch confidentiality,
   tokenization, stablecoins, enterprise adoption, compliance, settlement)? If low relevance, say skip.

2) THREE COMMENT ANGLES, each one or two sentences:
   - Angle A: add a sharp, specific perspective from the Ryle thesis (not a pitch).
   - Angle B: ask a genuine, pointed question that advances the conversation.
   - Angle C: extend their point with a concrete enterprise reality they may not have raised.

3) WHAT TO AVOID: one line flagging anything that would read as pitchy, generic, or off-tone here.

Rules: never pitch Ryle directly. No links. No hype or buzzwords. Sound like a peer with a view,
not a vendor. These are angles for the human to rewrite, not final text.
```

## Golden rules

- Never pitch the product in a comment. Add a view; let people find you.
- No links in comments.
- If the only honest angle is promotional, skip the post.
- Rewrite every angle in your own words before posting.
