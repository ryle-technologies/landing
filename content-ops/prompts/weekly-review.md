# Prompt: Weekly Review

A Friday ritual. Reviews the week's content and engagement, decides what to repeat and what to drop, and sets next week's single thesis post. Kept deliberately manual: at low volume there is no statistical signal for an "analytics agent" to learn from, and reading your own replies teaches you more than any classifier.

The most important metric is not likes. It is: **did this start a relevant conversation with a stablecoin/RWA/bank/infra/VC person?**

## How to use

1. Export or paste this week's rows from the Notion **Content DB** (what you posted, category, and the Outcome/Learning fields) plus a list of comments you made and any notable replies/DMs.
2. Provide the messaging spine, then this prompt, then that data.
3. File the output: update Learnings on rows, set next week's plan.

---

## Prompt

```
You are Ryle's content strategist running a weekly review. Ryle is confidential digital asset
infrastructure. Follow the attached MESSAGING SPINE.

I will paste this week's posts (with category and any outcome notes) and my engagement activity.
Judge quality by RELEVANT CONVERSATIONS STARTED with target personas (stablecoin, RWA,
bank, payments, infra, VC), not by likes. Produce:

1) WHAT WORKED: the 2-3 themes/posts that started real conversations, and why.

2) WHAT TO STOP: anything generic, off-voice, or that only drew vanity engagement.

3) REPURPOSE: pick the single best post and outline how to turn it into one longer
   LinkedIn post or article next week.

4) NEXT WEEK'S PLAN:
   - one thesis post (the anchor)
   - 2 supporting post ideas (use-case / founder-note)
   - 5 engagement targets (personas or specific voices worth commenting on)

5) ONE EXPERIMENT: a single thing to try differently next week.

Be honest and specific. If a week was weak, say so and diagnose why.
```

## Cadence note

Do this every Friday in ~20 minutes. Over time the "What worked / What to stop" notes become your real, earned content strategy — far more valuable than any automated metric dashboard at this stage.
